# ADR 0004: Recalibrate the FIA managed scenarios to FIADB working fractions

Status: ACCEPTED and IMPLEMENTED (baked into fullseries CSVs, deployed v0.64, 2026-06-04)
Date: 2026-06-04
Deciders: A. Weiskittel + PERSEUS team
Relates to: ADR 0003 (FIA hybrid+recal engine). Affects only the managed buckets.

## Context

The FIA managed scenarios (`yc_fia_empirical_v1`, `managed (harvest|intensive|
conservation)` and their disturbance/mortality variants) were generated by applying an
owner-typical harvest rotation to the ENTIRE land base of each owner at once. Marched
forward this removes about 1.9 percentage points of carbon per year, flipping the reserve
gain of +1.45 %/yr into a loss, and drove an implausible CONUS decline (managed harvest
-38%, intensive -65%) that disagreed with both the FIA record and the other engines.

The FIA growth-removal record shows the real landscape is very different: observed harvest
removal averages about 0.02 %/yr of standing carbon, because only ~0.8% of plots are cut
in a given remeasurement, and net carbon change is +1.1 %/yr. The whole-landscape rotation
was therefore roughly 100x too aggressive.

## Decision

Model each managed scenario as a partial-landscape blend with the reserve trajectory,
using FIADB-derived per-state working fractions:

    managed = phi * full_rotation + (1 - phi) * reserve

- `managed (harvest)` and `managed (conservation)`: phi = harvested_share (the membership
  harvest-treatment share, i.e. observed FIA harvest probability).
- `managed (intensive)`: phi = planted_share (STDORGCD = 1, plantations only; intensive
  management applies to the plantation estate).
- Reserved forest (RESERVCD majority) is excluded by construction (never harvested or
  planted), which matters most in the West (14 to 28% of forest).

Shares are computed by `ycx_mgmt_shares.R` (`docs/results/fia_mgmt_shares_bystate.csv`):
CONUS reserved 4.2%, planted 11.6%, harvested 7.1%; planted concentrates in the Southern
pine belt (FL 30%, GA 28%, MS/AL/LA ~26%), harvested in working-forest states (LA/MS/MT/
ID/ME 12 to 14%).

The recalibration is BAKED into the canonical FIA fullseries CSVs on Cardinal
(`ycx_bake_managed_csv.py`), so FIA re-injections (including CBM/GCBM engine ingests)
produce correct managed buckets with no post step. The whole-landscape rotation is kept
beside each file as `*.full.csv`. A JSON-level fallback (`ycx_blend_fia_datadriven.py`)
exists for processes that emit full-rotation JSON directly.

## Consequences

- CONUS carbon: reserve +63%, conservation +62%, harvest +62%, intensive +60%. At observed
  intensity, managed forests track reserves closely at the national scale.
- Differentiation concentrates where management is real: GA intensive +84% / FL +73% (pine
  plantations); ME harvest +27% (12% working forest); states without plantations show
  intensive near the reserve line.
- Persistent declines remain only in the disturbance-exposed variants (e.g. ME managed
  harvest disturbance-exposed -20%), correctly attributing downside to fire/insect/drought
  risk rather than routine harvest.
- The same blend is applied to `yc_hybrid_v1` and `yc_treemap_spatial_v1` managed buckets
  (post-injection) for within-bucket consistency.

## Caveats / future work

- phi uses the per-remeasurement harvested share as a static working fraction; a fuller
  treatment would model asynchronous rotation (landscape equilibrium) so high-harvest
  regions reach a flat steady state rather than a damped decline.
- Volume/merch mortality under the stress arms is mapped from the carbon GRM grid (the GRM
  lacks merch-resolution mortality); central trajectories do not depend on it.
